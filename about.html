The Spike Manifesto

A hedgehog. A dream. An unreasonable amount of pineapple pizza.

🌊 🦔
2017
The Escape

Spike quits his corporate job

Spike had a perfectly fine life in suburban Ohio. A small burrow. A steady job in middle management at a nut storage facility. A commute he described as "fine, I guess."

Then one Tuesday he watched a documentary about Hawaii and didn't stop thinking about it for six months. On a Thursday in March, he put in his notice, packed a single canvas tote, and bought a one-way ticket to Maui. His coworkers gave him a card. He did not read it until he was already on the plane.

2017
The Revelation

A hedgehog discovers pineapple pizza and loses his mind

On his third night in Maui, Spike ordered a Hawaiian pizza by accident (he was trying to order a plain cheese, but the laminated menu was confusing and he has small hands). It changed everything.

He ate the whole thing on the beach at sunset. The pineapple. The salt air. The fact that nobody in Ohio had let him live like this. He ordered another one. He has eaten pineapple pizza at least four times a week ever since. He has no regrets. He will not be debating this.

"I just thought — if I love linen shirts and pineapple pizza this much, other hedgehogs probably do too. Or humans. Whoever."

— Spike, Founder & Chief Hedgehog Officer
🛖 🦔
2019
The Business

Quill & Co opens, sort of

After two years of living on the beach, wearing linen, and thinking about it, Spike started making things. First a shirt with a hedgehog print. Then a tote. Then the hat. He sold them at a farmer's market in Kihei out of a wagon.

He had no website, no brand strategy, and no business plan. He did have extremely strong opinions about fabric weight and a surprisingly good eye for colour. People kept buying the shirt. He kept making it. Eventually someone said "you should put this online" and he did, and here we are.

Now
The Vibe

Still on the beach. Still eating pizza.

Quill & Co ships hedgehog-lifestyle goods to people all over the world. Spike still personally approves every product. He tests each linen shirt by wearing it to the beach and seeing if he feels good. He has not once felt bad.

The pineapple pizza print is the best-selling product. Spike is not surprised. Spike is never surprised anymore. He has found his peace.
